Community Cluster Style

How should communities appear at the overview level?

Colored Halos

Soft colored background behind each community's cluster. Community name as a label. Cross-community edges are bold dashed lines that clearly bridge the gap.

Dashed Borders

Communities bounded by dashed rectangles with rounded corners. Cleaner separation but more rigid — force layout needs constraints to keep nodes inside their boxes.

Color Gravity (No Borders)

No explicit boundaries. Nodes are colored by community, and the force simulation uses gravity wells to pull same-community nodes together. Communities emerge organically. Cross-community links naturally stretch across space.

All three make cross-community links prominent. The difference is how explicitly communities are bounded.
